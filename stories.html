Stories & Reflections

Shared with permission,
and with care.

The stories families share with us are deeply personal. We publish them carefully, with respect for the lives behind each memorial and with an understanding that remembrance looks different in every home.

Pet Memorial

"It felt like the first thing we had held onto that did not feel fragile."

After losing their older Labrador, one family chose a Memory Stone to keep beside his collar and lead. They told us the weight of it made the remembrance feel steadier and more peaceful in the weeks that followed.

Shared by a Melbourne family

Human Memorial

"We wanted something quieter than an urn, but more tangible than a photograph."

Created from an existing fingerprint held by a funeral home, this stone was made for siblings looking for a memorial that could sit naturally in the home without feeling ceremonial every day.

Shared by a family in regional Victoria

Partner Reflection

"Families appreciate being offered something personal without feeling pressured."

A veterinary clinic partner described Memory Stones as a gentle extension of aftercare: simple to explain, dignified to provide, and meaningful long after the appointment itself has passed.

Shared by a veterinary clinic partner

How Stories Are Shared

Privacy comes first.

We only share reflections that have been given to us with permission, and we keep details limited when families prefer their remembrance to stay private.

If You Prefer Privacy

A private enquiry is always welcome.

You do not need to share your story publicly to ask about a memorial. Many families prefer a quiet, direct conversation, and that is exactly what we are here for.

If you would prefer to speak privately about your own circumstances, we welcome that.
