# Memory Stones Content System

## Brand Voice Guidance

### Core tone

- Calm, elegant, and emotionally literate.
- Premium without sounding exclusive or cold.
- Reassuring without sounding clinical.
- Respectful of grief, private decision-making, and family dynamics.

### Writing principles

- Write to someone who may be overwhelmed, grieving, or short on emotional energy.
- Prefer gentle clarity over poetic excess.
- Answer practical questions directly.
- Replace hard-sell language with quiet confidence.
- Keep sentences breathable and well-spaced.
- Use restraint: fewer claims, better phrased.

### Avoid

- Hype, urgency, countdown language, or conversion clichés.
- Overpromising around timing or outcomes.
- Trendy brand language that makes the work feel commercialised.
- Generic luxury phrases like "the ultimate keepsake" or "one-of-a-kind experience."

### Useful phrasing patterns

- "If you are unsure what is possible, we can guide you."
- "Handled with care, privacy, and respect."
- "Created for families who want something tangible to hold onto."
- "A considered memorial, made quietly and well."

## FAQ

### When should I order an impression kit?

If you may want a print taken directly, it is best to order as early as possible. If a clinic or funeral home has already taken a print, we may be able to work from that instead.

### Can impressions be taken after passing?

Often, yes, though the timing varies with the circumstances. If you are unsure, please reach out as soon as you can and we will let you know what may still be possible.

### Do you work with vets and funeral homes?

Yes. We support both direct family orders and partner-facilitated memorials, including situations where a print has already been taken with care.

### What if I already have a print?

If you already have a usable paw print, fingerprint, or handprint, send us the details and we can confirm whether it appears suitable before you proceed.

### How long does production take?

Timeframes depend on whether we are waiting on a returned impression, reviewing engraving details, or beginning the final handmade piece. We aim to communicate clearly at each stage.

### Will every stone look exactly the same?

No. Each Memory Stone is individually handcrafted, and slight variation is part of the character of the memorial. The goal is to preserve the distinct impression, not to make something generic.

### Can I send one as a gift?

Yes, although many families prefer to arrange the memorial directly. If you are considering a gift, contact us first so we can advise on the most appropriate option.

### What happens if there is a problem with my order?

If there has been damage in transit or a genuine workmanship issue, contact us promptly and we will review the most appropriate resolution, including repair or remake where justified.

### Is my information handled privately?

Yes. Memorial details, print materials, and personal information should be handled with discretion. We use them only for the purpose of supporting and completing your order unless otherwise agreed.

### Do you ship across Australia?

Yes. We currently ship Australia-wide. If you need a delivery outside Australia, contact us before ordering so we can confirm what may be possible.

## Policy Page Positioning

### Shipping Policy

Keep the tone practical and clear. Emphasise that memorial products may involve multiple stages, that timeframes depend on impression return and production, and that the business will communicate honestly where timing matters.

### Returns / Remake Policy

State clearly that custom memorial items are generally not eligible for change-of-mind returns, while preserving Australian Consumer Law rights and offering a calm, fair remake pathway where workmanship issues arise.

### Privacy Policy

Acknowledge that memorial work often involves sensitive personal details, names, dates, and print materials. Emphasise discretion, limited use, and careful handling of information.

### Terms of Service

Keep these plain-English, modest in tone, and focused on custom work, supplied print materials, pricing, handmade variation, and lawful limits on liability.
